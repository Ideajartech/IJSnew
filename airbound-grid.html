What worked well

01

Brand Positioning

We repositioned Airbound from a "drone company" to India's most audacious hardware company, framing it as a category creator in logistics infrastructure. Instead of focusing on features, we amplified the mission: "Delivery at ₹0.10/km, built to make logistics invisible."

02

Audience Alignment

We mapped the audiences that mattered: engineers, researchers, early-stage collaborators, and visionary investors. Every content stream was tailored to speak their language: simplifying complex engineering breakthroughs while preserving technical credibility.

03

Founder Visibility

We turned Naman's philosophy of "engineering without compromise" into the voice of Airbound's vision. Through thought-leadership posts and first-principles storytelling, he began owning the deep-tech conversation online. Within 2 weeks, his content crossed 50K+ impressions.

04

Growth Engine

After validating traction during MVG, we scaled through Growth Marketing Solution. This phase included whitepapers, milestone-driven storytelling, thought-leadership campaigns, and real-time social coverage of Airbound's $8.65M funding launch event.

05

LinkedIn Marketing

We built a consistent marketing rhythm across both the founder's profile and Airbound's brand page. Founder-led content drove authenticity, while brand-led posts reinforced culture, authority, and scale, keeping engagement high and visibility consistent.

06

Market Validation

The results validated our clarity-driven approach. In a short span, Airbound's online presence became a magnet for high inbound interest—proof that deep-tech storytelling can scale without heavy ad spend, sparking conversations, hiring applications, and visibility.
