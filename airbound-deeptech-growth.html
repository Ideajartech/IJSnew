The Story

Through a combined MVG and Growth Marketing plan, Airbound transformed its founder-led engineering vision into a scalable brand narrative. In 3 months, the brand crossed 145K+ content reach and raised $8.65M funding.

Airbound is a deep-tech startup rewriting the rules of logistics. Founded by Naman Pushp, driven by engineering and first principles, the company is building zero-infrastructure delivery aircraft—engineered, manufactured, and flown in India for the world.

Naman's mission is simple: To make logistics invisible and affordable. He began working on this in 2020, at the age of 15, and has scaled the project into a capital-backed deep-tech team of 50+ folks. Airbound closed a notable seed round as it prepared to scale its manufacturing and go-to-market efforts.

When we began, that mission was buried in engineering decks and internal documents. The brand had an audacious vision but low visibility. Their content lived inside technical circles, and their founder's story wasn't reaching the right audience that could help them scale.

Like many early deep-tech startups, Airbound faced three core challenges:

  • Positioning gap: The story was too technical to travel beyond aerospace communities.
  • Visibility gap: No consistent narrative to build awareness or credibility.
  • Talent gap: Difficulty attracting top-tier engineers aligned with their vision.

We positioned Airbound as India's most visible deep-tech hardware company.

What worked well

01

Brand Positioning

We repositioned Airbound from a "drone company" to India's most audacious hardware company, framing it as a category creator in logistics infrastructure. Instead of focusing on features, we amplified the mission: "Delivery at ₹0.10/km, built to make logistics invisible."

02

Audience Alignment

We mapped the audiences that mattered: engineers, researchers, early-stage collaborators, and visionary investors. Every content stream was tailored to speak their language: simplifying complex engineering breakthroughs while preserving technical credibility.

03

Founder Visibility

We turned Naman's philosophy of "engineering without compromise" into the voice of Airbound's vision. Through thought-leadership posts and first-principles storytelling, he began owning the deep-tech conversation online. Within 2 weeks, his content crossed 50K+ impressions.

04

Growth Engine

After validating traction during MVG, we scaled through Growth Marketing Solution. This phase included whitepapers, milestone-driven storytelling, thought-leadership campaigns, and real-time social coverage of Airbound's $8.65M funding launch event.

05

LinkedIn Marketing

We built a consistent marketing rhythm across both the founder's profile and Airbound's brand page. Founder-led content drove authenticity, while brand-led posts reinforced culture, authority, and scale, keeping engagement high and visibility consistent.

06

Market Validation

The results validated our clarity-driven approach. In a short span, Airbound's online presence became a magnet for high inbound interest—proof that deep-tech storytelling can scale without heavy ad spend, sparking conversations, hiring applications, and visibility.
